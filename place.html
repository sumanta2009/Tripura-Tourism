UJJAYANTA PALACE
AGARTALA
Ujjayanta Palace, a royal residence of the former Tripura kings, is a stunning white marble palace surrounded by lush gardens. Now converted into a museum, it showcases Tripura's culture, lifestyle, and historical artifacts.
SUMANTA
NEERMAHAL
RUDRASAGAR LAKE
Neermahal, meaning "Water Palace," is a majestic structure in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake. Built as a summer retreat for the Tripura kings, it beautifully blends Hindu and Islamic architectural styles.

TRIPURA SUNDARI TEMPLE
UDAIPUR
Also known as Matabari, this temple is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas and is dedicated to Goddess Tripura Sundari. Its serene environment and spiritual significance attract devotees from across the country.

SEPAHIJALA WILDLIFE SANCTUARY
BISHALGARH
Spread over 18.53 sq. km, this sanctuary is home to exotic flora and fauna, including the famous spectacled monkey. It also houses a botanical garden, zoo, and boating facilities.
SUMANTA
UNAKOTI
KAILASHAHAR
Unakoti is an archaeological marvel with rock-cut sculptures of Hindu deities dating back to the 7th–9th centuries. Known as the "Angkor Wat of the Northeast," it holds cultural and historical significance.
SUMANTA
JAMPUI HILLS
NORTH TRIPURA
Renowned for its scenic beauty and panoramic views, Jampui Hills is a popular hill station. It is also famous for orange plantations and its Orange Festival.
SUMANTA
CHABIMURA
GOMATI DISTRICT
Known for its ancient rock carvings on steep mountain walls along the Gomati River, Chabimura is a hidden gem that offers a boat ride with breathtaking views.

BUDDHA TEMPLE (Buddha Mandir)
AGARTALA
This tranquil Buddhist shrine is an important spiritual destination. It showcases beautiful statues of Lord Buddha and attracts both tourists and devotees.

KAMALASAGAR LAKE
NEAR BANGLADESH BORDER
Kamalasagar Lake is a serene spot ideal for picnics and boating. The ancient Kali Temple nearby adds to the historical charm of the location.

HERITAGE PARK
AGARTALA
A beautifully maintained park that reflects Tripura’s cultural heritage through miniature models of historical sites. It’s a great place for relaxing and learning about the state’s rich history.

DUMBOOR LAKE
AMARPUR
Dumboor Lake is a picturesque water body shaped like a tabor drum. It is surrounded by hills and small islands, offering an excellent spot for boating and bird-watching.

PILAK
SOUTH TRIPURA
Pilak is an archaeological site with ancient Hindu and Buddhist sculptures. It reflects the cultural confluence of different religions in the region.

GUMTI WILDLIFE SANCTUARY
SOUTH TRIPURA
Spread over 389.54 sq. km, this sanctuary is a haven for elephants, bison, and deer. It also boasts a rich variety of birds, making it a paradise for nature enthusiasts.

MELAGHAR
NEAR NEERMAHAL
Melaghar is a charming town known for its festivals, particularly Durga Puja and the Boat Race Festival on Rudrasagar Lake.

ROWA WILDLIFE SANCTUARY
NORTH TRIPURA
This small sanctuary is a biodiversity hotspot, home to several rare species of flora and fauna. It is an ideal destination for eco-tourists and researchers.
